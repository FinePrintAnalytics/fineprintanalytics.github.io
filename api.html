Basic Endpoints — Free

Get Team Records
GET/v1/teams/record
Basic · Free
Returns team win/loss records by season, including home record, away record, and overall win percentage. Defaults to the current season. Filter by team_id for a single team or leave blank to retrieve the full league. Useful as a foundation for any model that weights team quality or home/away splits.
Use Case

Quickly assess how a team performs at home vs on the road before evaluating a spread. A team with a strong home record but a losing away record is a very different bet depending on location — this endpoint gives you that split instantly without building your own tracking database.

Parameters
NameTypeExampleDescription
team_idstringDETBasketball Reference team abbreviation. Optional — omit for all teams.
seasonnumber2026Season year. Defaults to current season.
Get Team Pace
GET/v1/teams/pace
Basic · Free
Returns game pace and possession data by team, including average possessions per game and pace ranking relative to the league. One of the most underutilized signals in betting — pace mismatch between a fast team and a slow team has a measurable effect on game totals and scoring patterns.
Use Case

When a high-pace team like OKC plays a slow, defensive team like NYK, the total line often doesn't fully account for the pace suppression. Pull both teams' pace profiles, calculate the mismatch, and compare against the posted total. This endpoint is the data layer behind one of the strongest signals in the Fine Print model.

Get Team Rest
GET/v1/teams/rest
Basic · Free
Returns rest days and back-to-back flags per game by team. Includes days since last game for both home and away teams, and flags whether the game is part of a back-to-back set. Rest differential is a classic betting signal — particularly valuable when one team is on a back-to-back and the other has had 2+ days off.
Use Case

An away team playing their second game in two nights while the home team has had three days off is a significant structural disadvantage. Use this endpoint to systematically identify those spots across the full slate rather than relying on manually checking schedules. Combine with travel data (Pro tier) for an even stronger signal.

Pro Endpoints — $49/mo

Get Referee Tendencies
GET/v1/referees/tendencies
Pro
Returns historical referee game tendencies including average pace, total fouls called, free throw rate, and home team win percentage. Referee assignment is one of the most underpriced signals in NBA betting — certain officials consistently call faster or slower games, favor home teams, or allow more physical play. Defaults to refs with 20+ games; use min_games=1 to see all referees.
Use Case

If Scott Foster is assigned and he has a 68% home win rate in his career sample, that's a structural edge for the home team regardless of who's playing. Pull tonight's referee assignments, cross-reference their home win tendencies, and factor that into your model before the line moves. This kind of data is what sharp bettors pay for.

Parameters
NameTypeExampleDescription
referee_namestringScott FosterFull referee name. Optional — omit for all referees.
min_gamesnumber20Minimum games officiated. Defaults to 20 to filter small samples.

If tonight's ref has a persistent over lean and the total is sitting on a key number, that's actionable. Use this alongside team pace data to build a complete picture of expected scoring environment.

A West Coast team playing a 1pm ET road game on the East Coast is essentially playing at 10am their body time. Over 3+ seasons of data, teams in this spot cover at a significantly lower rate than the market implies. Use the lag advantage score to filter for the highest-impact timezone spots without manually tracking every team's travel schedule.

Notes & Defaults

Referee Filters
Referee endpoints default to officials with 20+ games to filter small samples. Pass min_games=1 to include all referees regardless of sample size.
Player Filters
Player/referee endpoints default to a minimum of 10 games together. Use min_games=1 to see all combinations including small samples.
Historical Access
Historical seasons 2023–2025 require Ultra tier. Pass ?season=2023 through ?season=2025 for historical data. Basic and Pro default to current season only.
